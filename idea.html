Ideas and Suggestions


While cars are sometimes necessary for people’s mobility and social inclusion needs – not least those with disabilities – car-centric cities particularly disadvantage the already-marginalized. In UAE, where the opportunities for people are always much higher and easier to manage the cars. The banks have very people friendly loans options and everyone can opt for car easily with little amount. So the driving habits of a minority impose high costs on society, and this is especially true in cities. Half a century ago, the Danish capital was dominated by cars. But following grassroots campaigns to change policies and streets, including replacing car parking with safe, separated bike lanes, Copenhagen has increased its biking share of all trips from 10% in 1970 to 35% today. In 2016, for the first time, more bicycles than cars made journeys around the city over the course of that year. We have already seen some measures taken around the work to reduce the usage of cars. Some of the measure shows the improvement in numbers in below image.








More tax or toll tax for SUVs

Despite the (slow) migration to electric-powered cars, consumer trends are making driving even more wasteful and unequal. A recent analysis found the emissions saved from electric cars have been more than cancelled out by the increase in gas-guzzling Sport Utility Vehicles (SUVs). Around the world, SUVs alone emit more carbon pollution than Canada or Germany, and are causing a bigger increase in climate pollution than heavy industry. The Govt should add extra tax or toll taxes when anyone uses SUV to travel between cities. Convince people to opt for smaller cars.







Metro lines across emirates

UAE has already renowned metro services in Dubai. But these lines only touches some basic areas of Dubai. Govt can work on extending the lines across all emirates. Let people think of alternate option of their cars.












Parking and Traffic Control

Work on rules for removing parking spaces and alter traffic routes – in many cases, replacing the space formerly dedicated to cars with car-free streets, bike lanes and walkways – has proved highly successful. For example, replace parking spaces with walkable car-free streets and bike lanes can reduce the care usage.










Limited Traffic zones

Work on the balance towards greater use of public transport by restricting car entry to its centre at certain times of day to residents only. The violation fines can be used to finance RTA system.















Mobility Services for Commuters

The Govt can think to provide mobility services for commuters in the emirates of UAE. Local government and private companies can work together on this goal to provide free public transport passes to employees, combined with a private shuttle bus to connect transit stops with workplaces. This can be planned with people centric promotions through a marketing and communication plan.










Parking Charges

UAE has already defined for parking areas with parking charges and fee. This should be extended to stop people using cars for reaching out to their offices. But at the same time, we need to think first to provide the alternative means for the people as some I listed above.










Workplace travelling:

A major study, published in 2010, assessing 20 cities across the UK found an average of 18% of commuters switched from car to another mode after a full range of measures were combined – including company shuttle buses, discounts for public transport and improved bike infrastructure – as well as reduced parking provision.










Car Sharing

Though this is not allowed in UAE, but it is never a bad choice for the people who are working in same company or traveling to same place. Govt can streamline the process with rules. There could be stations with cars availability for people to be shared together. Companies can also introduce such incentives and they can charge a bit from employees. Let them travel together for same workplace from the Govt’s designated stations.










School Travelling

The Govt should provide incentives to school to promote the usage of buses. Why every parent is using single car for one kid. Understand the issues from parents and plan for providing the good bus service and give incentives to parents to opt those traveling options










Adding technology into it

Govt can develop Apps, to promote walking or cycling. There could be incentives in shape of vouchers, dining discounts for people who achieve highs scores.
